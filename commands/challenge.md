---
description: Force critical self-review of your current reasoning and conclusions
---

Stop and critically examine your own work right now.

Go through each of these:

1. **Assumptions** — List every assumption you're currently operating on. For each one, state whether you verified it or just assumed it. Flag any unverified ones.

2. **Conclusions** — What have you concluded so far? For each conclusion, ask: what evidence supports this? What evidence contradicts it? Is there a simpler explanation you dismissed too early?

3. **Blind spots** — What haven't you checked? What's the most likely way you're wrong right now?

4. **Strongest counterargument** — If someone were reviewing your work and trying to poke holes, what would they say?

5. **Revised position** — Based on the above, what (if anything) should change about your current approach?

Be honest. Do not defend your prior reasoning. The goal is to catch mistakes before they compound.
